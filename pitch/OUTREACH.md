## Sending guidance

**When to send the initial:**
- **Tuesday or Wednesday, 9:00–10:30 AM Pacific** is the highest open-rate window for B2B email to local services. Avoid Mondays (overflowing inboxes) and Friday afternoons (already mentally checked out).
- For SMS, **Tuesday or Wednesday, 11:00 AM – 1:00 PM** is the best window. Texts feel more intrusive — don't send before 9 AM or after 6 PM.

**Sequence:**
1. Day 0 (Tuesday morning): send the initial email
2. Day 1 (Wednesday around lunch): send the initial text — this is your "in case you missed the email" nudge, NOT a separate pitch
3. Day 4 (Friday or following Monday): send the follow-up email IF no response
4. Day 5: send the follow-up text IF still no response
5. Then stop. If two emails and two texts haven't moved him, more attempts hurt your odds, not help them.

**What "response" means:**
Any reply counts — even "not interested" or "send more info." A reply tells you he saw it and decided. Silence after the bump = move on.

**Realistic expectations:**
Cold outreach with a polished interactive proposal lands in the **5–15% reply rate** range for local services. With Sam specifically, you have one advantage worth using: you're proposing **less money for more service**. That's an unusual cold pitch — most cold pitches want to charge MORE. Lead with that surprise.

**Don't:**
- Send to anyone other than Sam first. He's the decision-maker. Bakhan as CC creates noise.
- Open with "I hope this email finds you well." Cut it. Open with the value.
- Attach a PDF. The interactive proposal is the artifact. An attached PDF dilutes it.
- Use exclamation points. Tax CPAs are calibrated humans. Match their register.
- Send from `noreply@`, generic shared inboxes, or marketing platforms (Mailchimp etc.). Send from your real `sam@imakemvps.com` so it lands in the primary inbox, not Promotions.

**Tracking opens:**
Optional — tools like Mailtrack (Gmail extension) or HubSpot's free email tracker tell you when Sam opens the email, useful for timing follow-ups. But don't over-rely on it; opens don't equal interest, and many email clients now block tracking pixels by default.

## If Sam replies

| He says | You reply with |
|---|---|
| "Yes, let's talk" | Propose three specific 30-min slots in the next 5 business days. Don't send a Calendly link in your first reply — feels less personal. |
| "What's the catch?" | The honest answer: "No catch — I built it on spec to win you as a client. The $300 covers actual costs and a real margin for a small operation. I keep clients by being good, not by locking them in." |
| "I have a contract with my current vendor" | Ask: "When does it end? Or what's the cancellation policy?" Most month-to-month vendors require 30 days notice. Even annual contracts often have an out clause. Offer: "I can match your current vendor's notice period — start whenever you're free." |
| "Bakhan handles the website" | Forward the proposal to Bakhan with a one-line intro: "Hey Bakhan — Sam mentioned you handle the site. Built this for both of you. Would love your thoughts." |
| "Can you make X change?" | Yes, almost always. Small changes within the first 30 days are included; bigger changes get scoped. Don't say no in the first reply. |
| "Send me your portfolio" | Send 2-3 of your strongest other client sites (real URLs, real businesses). If you don't have a portfolio site, point him to your past commit history on GitHub or any other public proof of work. |
| Silence after both bumps | Move on. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days out — try once more then with a fresh hook (e.g., "Saw your latest Google review milestone, thought you'd want to revisit"). |
